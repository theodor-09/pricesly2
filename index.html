Our Cause

Globally, over 1 billion tonnes of edible food are wasted each year—driven by household and retail inefficiencies—which amounts to $1.6 trillion in losses, leaves one‐third of the world food insecure, and generates 8–10% of greenhouse gas emissions. This waste stems from time‐consuming manual tracking, human error, and a lack of real‐time inventory insights at both the household (631 M tonnes, $1 trillion loss) and retail (131 M tonnes, $400 billion loss) levels.

This is because:

  • Consumers do not have the capacity or time to optimize their groceries in accordance with their goals and multitude of options
  • Retailers still struggle to some extent to predict consumer demand

Pricesly wishes to remove:

Human Error

Food Waste
